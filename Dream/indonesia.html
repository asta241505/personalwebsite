Indonesia

Bali has always been on my travel bucket list because of its breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and relaxing tropical vibes. Known as the "Island of the Gods," Bali offers a perfect mix of adventure, tranquility, and spiritual experiences. From pristine beaches to ancient temples, the island has so much to offer, making it an ideal destination for exploration and relaxation.

How to Go There

To get to Indonesia from the Philippines, you can take a direct flight from Manila (NAIA) or Cebu (Mactan-Cebu) to major Indonesian cities like Jakarta or Bali (Ngurah Rai International Airport). Airlines such as Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and Garuda Indonesia offer regular flights. The flight duration is around 3 to 4 hours, making it a convenient destination for travelers from the Philippines.

What I Want to See & Experience:

Nusa Dua Beach

Nusa Dua Beach is one of the top destinations I want to visit in Bali because of its pristine white sand, crystal-clear waters, and luxurious atmosphere. Known for its tranquil surroundings and high-end resorts, this beach offers the perfect balance between relaxation and adventure.

Tanah Lot Temple

Tanah Lot Temple is one of the most iconic and breathtaking destinations in Bali, and visiting this place is high on my list. This temple, dramatically perched on a rock formation in the sea, offers a unique cultural and spiritual experience combined with stunning natural beauty.

Padang Padang Beach

Padang Padang Beach is a hidden paradise in Bali, known for its crystal-clear waters, soft golden sand, and stunning limestone cliffs. I want to visit this beach to experience its relaxing tropical vibe, swim in its calm turquoise waters, and explore its unique rock formations. It's also famous as a surfing spot, and while I may not be a pro surfer, I’d love to watch the waves and enjoy the lively beach atmosphere.

Bali Swing in Ubud

The Bali Swing in Ubud offers a thrilling experience with breathtaking jungle views. I want to visit this spot to feel the adrenaline rush of swinging high above the lush rainforest, with stunning rice terraces, rivers, and palm trees below.It’s the perfect place for unforgettable photos and a unique way to embrace Bali’s natural beauty.

Sekumpul Waterfall

Sekumpul Waterfall is known as one of the most beautiful waterfalls in Bali, surrounded by lush greenery and stunning natural scenery. I want to visit this breathtaking spot to witness the twin cascading falls, feel the refreshing mist, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere of Bali’s untouched nature.
